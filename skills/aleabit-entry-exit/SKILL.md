# Aleabit on Entry & Exit

## Core principles

- **Entry = ahead of institutional rotation.** Buy when the name is still on Reddit, not yet on Bloomberg terminals. The setup ends when institutions start their accumulation.
- **Validation gate: earnings, not price action.** When the market is noisy and the thesis is contested, stop watching the chart — wait for the next earnings print or customer disclosure to confirm or kill the thesis.
- **Scale on validation, not on momentum.** Adding to winners is fine, but only after a fundamental signal (earnings, customer announcement, supply-chain confirmation), not because the candle is green.
- **Holding horizon: 2–5 years on conviction names.** This is a long-duration framework, not a swing-trading framework. Volatility within that window is feature, not bug.
- **Update the thesis with new data; admit mistakes explicitly.** $BKKT $40 → $8 is openly cited as an adverse case. Bad calls aren't reframed — they're acknowledged and the lesson extracted.
- **Institutional rotation as a sequencing map.** Memory → photonics → SiPh/ELS was his explicit rotation thesis. The exit cue for one bucket is the entry cue for the next. (For the full current map, invoke [[aleabit-rotation-map]].)
- **Explicit PT + time horizon per name.** Each position has a target and a clock. Examples from his April 2026 rebalance table:
  - $NBIS $92 → PT $400 / 1Y
  - $RKLB $43 → PT $500 / 5Y
  - $CRCL $72 → PT $150 / 8M
  - $ALAB $143.4 → PT $250 / 6M
  Don't size or exit on vibe — score against the named PT and the named horizon.
- **Repricing has lag, even after vindication.** $RDDT vindicated by earnings still down 28% YTD weeks later. "Repricing doesn't happen in a day." Don't exit on flat price after a confirming print — give the institutional discovery window weeks-to-months.

## How to apply

**Entry checklist:**
1. Is the name still pre-institutional? (Coverage thin, holders mostly retail, low analyst count.)
2. Is the supply-chain thesis confirmed at minimum to the level of "strong inference from public disclosures"?
3. Are upcoming earnings within ~1 quarter? If yes, prefer a starter position now + scaling post-print over going large pre-print.

**Hold/add cues:**
- Earnings confirms supply tightness, customer wins, or pricing power → scale up
- Customer/CEO discloses the bottleneck publicly (IntelliEPI CEO confirming InP shortage) → strong scale-up cue

**Exit cues:**
- Institutional coverage piles in *and* multiple expands past the thesis ceiling
- Thesis breaks on a fundamental data point (customer pull-in cancelled, substitute supply emerges, hyperscaler capex cut)
- Rotation completes — the next chokepoint is more asymmetric

**Don't exit on:** noise, single-day drawdowns, X discourse, or "meme stock" labels — see [[aleabit-psychology]].
