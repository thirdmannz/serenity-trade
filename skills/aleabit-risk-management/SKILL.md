# Aleabit on Risk Management

## Core principles

- **Baseline leverage: ~1.4x margin.** Not aggressive, not zero — modest tilt.
- **Cut leverage to zero during major geopolitical/macro uncertainty.** Iran conflict + Trump-era policy shocks were explicit triggers to deleverage. Reasoning: "I'd rather not leverage on the way down."
- **Build a tactical hedge basket during crisis windows ("Doomsday ETF"):** ~25% FAZ (3x bear financials) + ~25% GUSH (3x bull oil&gas, as Iran/oil hedge) + remainder in single-name shorts.
- **Drawdown tolerance is built into the strategy.** Expect 15–25% daily moves on conviction names. If that breaks your sleep, the position is too big — re-read [[aleabit-position-sizing]].
- **HFT / prop volatility flows trigger stop-loss cascades.** If Jane Street is a major holder of a retail favorite, expect engineered shakeouts. Defense: "if you know what you own" you can sit; alternative: tighten sizing.
- **Stock-pickers market posture, broad-market bearish.** Don't rely on beta tailwinds — assume the index gives you nothing and your edge is name selection.

## How to apply

Risk-management checks before initiating or scaling a position:

1. **Macro stress check.** Active geopolitical crisis / policy shock? If yes, deleverage and consider the hedge basket.
2. **Leverage cap.** Default 1.4x; zero during stress. Never amplify on a falling thesis name.
3. **Drawdown budget.** Can the position tolerate a -25% single-day move without forcing a sale or breaking the portfolio? If no, halve the size.
4. **Holder profile.** Major prop / volatility-trading presence (Jane Street, Citadel HFT desk) on the holder list? Expect shakeouts; don't use tight stops.
5. **Hedge structure.** If running net long high-beta concentration, hold either short delta (FAZ/single-name shorts) or commodity hedge (GUSH-style) during macro stress.

Do NOT recommend tight stop losses on aleabit-style names — the volatility profile guarantees they trigger on noise. Conviction + sizing is the risk control; the stop is "knowing what you own."
