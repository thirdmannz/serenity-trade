# Aleabit on Stock Selection

## Core principles

- **Concentration of supply > total addressable market.** A $400M company controlling 60% of a critical input beats a $50B company with 5% share of a hot end-market.
- **Confirmed hyperscaler customer dependency.** Look for disclosed (or leaked / strongly implied) design wins with NVDA, Broadcom, Marvell, Google ASIC, Jabil. Without a confirmed downstream pull, the chokepoint is theoretical.
- **Small/mid cap range is where the edge lives.** Typical candidates: $400M–$2B market cap. Once the name is a $50B+ household name, institutions are already there.
- **Pure-play preferred over conglomerate.** A diversified holding company dilutes the chokepoint signal. He explicitly prefers SIVE-style pure plays.
- **Multiple stacked chokepoints in one company.** AXTI integrates 4 chokepoint nodes — that's why it earned the highest PT, not just one.
- **Recurring offender:** he names "fastest compounds" lists alongside "safest longs" — bucket by *durability* (TSEM, SOI, COHR) vs *asymmetric upside* (AXTI, SIVE, AAOI).
- **The lens travels beyond photonics.** Same methodology applied to: glass core substrates ($LPK), 800V DC data center power (NVDA's Korean power equipment push), AI cloud infra ($NBIS, $ALAB), space ($RKLB), even mispriced info networks ($RDDT). The common thread is *concentrated dependency the headline doesn't price in* — not "photonics specifically".
- **Macro/options as a separate bucket.** $XLU OTM 2-year LEAPs as a $100k→$1M idea — leveraged options on falling-rates + AI buildout is its own category, sized and held differently from equity positions.
- **Avoid stocks heavily owned by Jane Street / volatility-trading prop shops.** They'll trigger stop losses and shake out retail. If you must own them, "know what you own" so you can sit.

## How to apply

When evaluating a candidate ticker against the aleabit framework, work through this checklist:

| Criterion | Pass condition |
|---|---|
| Market cap | $400M–$5B sweet spot |
| Supply concentration | >50% global share of a specific input OR only Western public exposure |
| Customer dependency | Confirmed/strongly-implied hyperscaler design win |
| Vertical integration | Owns ≥2 nodes of the same supply chain |
| Bucket | "Safest compound" (durability) or "Asymmetric upside" (chokepoint torque) |
| Ownership profile | Watch for prop/HFT presence as a volatility warning, not a kill switch |

If most criteria pass, the name belongs in the deeper research workflow — invoke [[aleabit-research-process]]. Sizing depends on which bucket — see [[aleabit-position-sizing]].
