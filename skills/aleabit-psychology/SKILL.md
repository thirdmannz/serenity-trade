# Aleabit on Psychology & Conviction

## Core principles

- **Earliest = most criticized. Treat doubt as a signal, not a verdict.** When you're first on a name, the loudest voices will mock it. That's the price of being early.
- **The "meme stock" label is often noise from people whose models don't accommodate fundamental change.** If the chokepoint is real, the label is irrelevant. If the chokepoint isn't real, the label was right by accident — own the distinction.
- **Be combative on the thesis, humble on the data.** Push back hard on surface-level criticism. But admit specific factual errors explicitly and update.
- **Anti-noise discipline.** When X discourse gets loud, stop watching X and wait for earnings. The information density of social discourse is near zero compared to a 10-Q.
- **Conviction is built from supply-chain understanding, not from social validation.** If your thesis is "I bought because @somebody said so," the first 20% drawdown will shake you out. If your thesis is "I mapped this chokepoint and the only Western public exposure is X," you can sit.
- **Mistakes are publicly cited, not hidden.** The $BKKT $40 → $8 loss is openly referenced; "I underestimated Trump a bit on Iran" on the leverage call. Owning losses preserves credibility for the next call.
- **Anti-copy-trade discipline.** Don't blindly copy positions from anyone — including him. He explicitly doesn't announce sells, so followers can be left holding exit liquidity. If your conviction comes from someone else's tweet, you don't have conviction.
- **Calibrate expectations: 10%/day is not the baseline.** "If $SOI or $HPS.A are not up 10% a day people are sad." Recency-anchored expectations are the fastest path to bad sells.
- **Patience on mispricing.** A correct thesis confirmed by earnings can still take weeks-to-months to reprice. Don't sell flat after a good print just because the chart hasn't moved.

## How to apply

When a user is wavering during a drawdown or under social pressure:

1. **Re-anchor on the thesis primitives.** What is the chokepoint? Has any fact in that primitive changed? If no, the price movement is noise.
2. **Distinguish thesis breakage from price discomfort.** Drawdown ≠ thesis failure. A new substitute supplier, cancelled customer order, or hyperscaler capex cut = thesis failure. -25% on no news = noise.
3. **Stop reading X / Reddit during the drawdown window.** It is structurally net-negative for decision quality.
4. **Calendar the next earnings print and pre-commit:** "If [specific data point] shows up, I scale. If [specific data point] is missing, I exit." Pre-commitment beats real-time emotional response.
5. **Audit the source of conviction.** If conviction came from a tweet, it was never conviction — it was social proof. Either rebuild from supply-chain mapping ([[aleabit-research-process]]) or exit.

When recommending action, prioritize the position-sizing fix ([[aleabit-position-sizing]]) over a behavioral one — if a user is panicking, the position is usually too big.
