# Aleabit on Supply Chain Chokepoints

## Core principles

- **Don't buy the obvious beneficiary** — buy what the beneficiary *must* depend on. Skip NVDA/MSFT/META/GOOGL; hunt the narrow nodes they cannot route around.
- **Look for monopoly or duopoly control of a physical input.** AXTI + Sumitomo controlling ~60–70% of global InP substrates is the archetype.
- **Map the full dependency chain.** Raw material → substrate → epi-wafer → laser → transceiver → switch → ASIC. A real chokepoint sits at a node where substitution takes years, not quarters.
- **Vertical integration of multiple chokepoints under one company is the bullseye.** AXTI integrates 4 chokepoints in one supply chain — that's why he assigned a $150 PT from $12–15.
- **"Pure play" public exposure beats diversified conglomerates.** SIVE is the only public CW/EML laser pure play — that scarcity itself is the edge.
- **Earliest = most doubted.** When the chokepoint is real but the market still calls it a meme, you're early, not wrong.

## How to apply

When the user asks about an AI-themed stock, don't anchor on the headline name. Ask:
1. What physical input does the headline name depend on?
2. Who supplies that input, and how concentrated is supply?
3. Is there a *Western* public-market way to own that node?
4. Is the company vertically integrated across multiple bottleneck nodes?
5. Has institutional coverage started yet, or is it still retail/Reddit-only?

If the answer to (3) and (5) point at a small/mid cap with concentrated supply control, that's an "aleabit-style" candidate worth deeper supply chain mapping — invoke [[aleabit-research-process]] next.
